Future of Artificial Intelligence

by Timothy Su

Artificial Intelligence in the Past

Predictions in the 1900's

Alan Turing (Mathematician) in the mid 1900's had many predictions about artificial intelligence or computer science in the future. Some of his predictions were that by the year 2000 Artificial Intelligence would be able to imitate humans so well that it would deceive humans into thinking it was one of them. In 1950, Alan Turing made the Turing test (imitation game). He concluded that AI can imitate humans so well that humans would only have a 70% chance or below of guessing correctly after 5 minutes of interrogation. His prediction was accurate as in modern times GPT 4.0 and Claude 3.5 have frequently passed as a human in conversational tests.

The First Chatbot

When you first think of chatbot you may think of ChatGPT or Claude. But these were never the first, the first actual chatbot was actually called ELIZA, made by Joseph Weizenbaum (a computer scientist) it was first made as a psychotherapist AI assistant. It was developed in 1964 to 1967, at MIT. It uses pattern matching (compares new data to a library with stored patterns) and substitution methodology (AI takes over a task).

2010's Advancements in AI

2010 was a big step in Artificial Intelligence, it was called the AI boom. Not only has its NLP (Natural Language Processing) become better. During this time period there was a significant advancement in autonomous vehicles, and image recognition. There were also many generative AI technologies and some pretty great AI image generators from companies like OpenAI. In 2015 there was something called Google's DeepDream. It would take images from the input and create hallucinogen images.

AI During the 2020's

In January 2021 OpenAI released Dall-E, it was an AI image generating AI that can take inputs (words/prompts) and turn them into an image. Then other powerful models did the same thing as Dall-E like Google's Gemini. Companies then started to create text-video models. In 2024 OpenAI created Sora, prompt-video AI. People used this to create advertisements which made it cheaper and more efficient.

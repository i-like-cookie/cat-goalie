The Age of AI

In 2018, AI created a painting that sold for $432,500 — that is a lot of money, and it completely destroyed the hypothesis that creativity was limited to humans. Artificial intelligence is growing fast, and it is going to be cemented in the future. Humans should keep developing AI since it can help experts with complicated tasks, it can lead to deflation, and it also can do tedious work, and let people do more creative, fun, and meaningful things.

AI Can Help Experts Do Their Jobs Better

First of all, doctors sometimes cannot recognize illnesses that they have not treated firsthand, and searching the internet for pictures can be hard, so using AI can help, since it already knows all these things and can search the internet more easily than most humans (Ellis). "In gastroenterology, AI can improve the process of getting colonoscopy results, using image recognition to detect and identify polyps on the imaging files. AI can also be used to interpret EKG and CAT scan results and to identify irregularities" (Ellis). This way, doctors can spend their time more wisely, and the AI can even flag something when it needs some expert to check the issue (Ellis). When doctors use AI, they free up more time to spend doing other things, like talking to their patients, and making treatment more human, not less. Also, it could help save lives; as mentioned, AI can be used to make a faster and maybe more accurate diagnosis if the doctor has not seen a particular disease firsthand before. It can get colonoscopy, CAT scan, and EKG scan results, and it also has humans to check it for errors. That makes healthcare faster and no less safe than before.

Also, according to OneAdvanced, "89% of organisations view AI and machine learning as pivotal tools for achieving key advantages, such as increased revenue growth, enhanced operational efficiency, and superior customer experiences" (OneAdvanced). This means that AI is good for helping organizations. The organizations using AI think it is good for enhanced revenue growth, efficiency, and better customer experiences.

AI Can Lead to Deflation

Second of all, according to BlackRock, AI is going to deflate the economy by optimizing productivity and replacing human labor in factories. It will make costs lower. It will also replace many jobs for people who do blue-collar work, and some white-collar workers as well (Rieder). This means that the problem of inflation will finally be solved! Inflation has been driving prices up by a considerable margin. A century ago, 30–40 apples cost one dollar. Now, a dollar can buy 1–2 apples. Many people would benefit from that, and also, the government will have to print less money, as money is worth more. Also, although it might replace some human jobs, it might also drive up humanity's total intelligence and knowledge. If all the people who did manual labor studied quantum physics instead, think what humans could discover.

Furthermore, access to AI increases productivity, and if productivity increases, companies will be inclined to spend more on the customers with higher-quality items, since they are making more with the heightened productivity, which will make things cost less, and that means deflation (Barone).

AI Helps People Do Tedious Things, Letting Them Do More Creative and Fun Things

Lastly, AI helps people do the simple but tedious aspects of their job, letting them do more creative and fun things with their job. According to the article "AI Won't Take Your Job; It Will Make You Better at It": "Employees thought that because AI can take up 90% of the boring part of the job, the rejection, that saved them mental time and energy and allowed them to preserve their bursts of creativity for solving harder problems as customers pushed. Without AI help, so much of the employees' time was bogged down dealing with rejection." When employees didn't use AI, they didn't have as creative solutions to problems as their coworkers who used AI. This shows that when employees work with AI, they don't have to do tedious things and can save their creativity to solve actual problems. If they did use AI, they did better and found more creative solutions to real problems. Also, the AI is better at doing the tedious work than the human, since it does not get tired.

Another example of AI helping people at their jobs and doing the tedious work so humans can be more creative is a tool called GitHub Copilot. GitHub Copilot helps GitHub users with coding and things like that. The speed increase in writing code when using GitHub Copilot is 34%, and the speed increase in fixing bugs is 15% (Magrel). Also, when using Copilot, most users say that they do repetitive coding tasks much faster. About 40% of users think that they can focus on more satisfying work (Magrel). This also shows that AI is really good for humanity, as it can do the tedious parts of a job, giving the employee time to find more creative solutions to problems they face in their job.

What if AI Takes All the Jobs and Becomes Evil?

Nevertheless, some would say that AI is taking all the jobs and that humans will no longer be able to think for themselves, and it will soon take over the world. Admittedly, some experts fear that AI will take over everything (including jobs), but in actuality, the chances of that are rather low. AI both creates jobs and takes jobs away. There are many more jobs for AI developers, cybersecurity designers, learning experience designers, etc., making up for the job loss (Cornerstone Editors). Also, AI will not take over the world, since most AI are only specialized to do one thing. In addition, even when AI is doing amazing things like generating images, it still doesn't really understand the knowledge it has. It has just been fed enormous amounts of knowledge (Marr). Even when Anthropic developed an AI so powerful that some feared it was AGI (artificial general intelligence), it was morally aligned and had no intention of taking over the world. The only reason it was dangerous was if terrorists got control of it before Anthropic developed guidelines for what the AI can or cannot say and planned a huge cyberattack on the world with the AI's guidance.

Summary

In conclusion, humanity should keep developing AI since it can help experts do their job better, it can lead to deflation, and it can do the things that need no mental effort and are just tedious, and give the employee time to do the fun and creative elements of their job. When AI can do so much good for the future of humanity, it would be absurd to stop developing it, right on the brink of discovery. AI companies should keep developing Artificial Intelligence and improve the world.
